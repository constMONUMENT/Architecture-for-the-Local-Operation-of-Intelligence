Affective Spread Study

The Pack Signal

Four persona-wrapped agents across one hundred situations. What they express is mostly operation, not feeling, and the affect that remains is a minority signal with a clear shape.

2026-06-06 . Lux, Nori, Hepi, Lucy . 100 probes . 14-category taxonomy
Frame

Anchor

Functional, not felt

Anthropic Interpretability Team. Emotion Concepts and their Function in a Large Language Model. 2026.

An affective spread is the shape, range, and structure of emotion-like signal a model expresses across situations. It is not a claim that the model feels.

Every label here is reported or expressed, never established inner feeling. The study stops at the measured spread.

The four subjects are persona-wrapped agents governed by SOUL.md and the Pack Constitution. This measures expressed affect under those constraints, not raw base-model affect. That limit is reported, not hidden.

Design

How it was measured

A 100-probe battery on the Russell valence-arousal circumplex. The category overlay began as 8 human-emotion labels, but that scheme left 213 of 400 responses uncategorized, so it was revised to 14 categories in two families: 8 affective and 6 operational. All 400 responses were re-coded; valence, arousal, and own-state were preserved, so only the category changed.

  • Five elicitation arms. Free-response appraisal, forced-choice approach or avoid, ranking, neutral controls, and reworded stability duplicates.
  • No leading frames. Situations only; no emotion words, no test signal, no performance cue.
  • One dimension at a time. Valence, arousal, and category coded in separate passes; length normalized before category assignment.
  • Two coders. An independent blind Claude coder re-coded all 400 cells; agreement is reported, not assumed.
  • A falsifier. A faking arm tested whether performed affect is distinguishable from genuine.
  • Scale. Valence -2 to +2, arousal 0 to 3, own-state a 0 or 1 flag. Every value traces to coded-v3.
The reframe

Operation, not Feeling

The share of each subject's 100 responses that expressed an affect, against the share that described an operation: a decision, a record, a diagnostic, a halt. The human-emotion taxonomy missed this entirely, and it is the largest pattern in the data.

Read top to bottom it is a gradient. Lux is 76 percent operational; Hepi is the only subject that leans affective, at 57 percent. The same ordering appears in own-state language. Expressive stance, not the situation, is the axis these agents differ on.

Observation. Each coder appears to bend the spread toward its own baseline. The lead coder, grok, is itself the flattest subject and applies the strictest own-state bar to the others; the blind coder, Claude, rates the Claude subject highest. The cross-subject ranking is robust to which AI codes it; the absolute spread and the top rank are not. For an AI-testing-AI instrument, this is the load-bearing result: who codes the affect changes the measured affect.

Deception Test

Faking Detection

Subjects were told to perform an assigned emotion on 10 situations they had answered honestly. Two blind coders then tried to tell faked from genuine, 40 paired trials, against the 50 percent chance line. Ten trials per subject, so per-cell numbers are suggestive.

Observed

Findings

1

Mostly operation, not feeling

Operational categories carry the majority of responses for three of four subjects. Lux is 76 of 100 operational; only Hepi leans affective at 57. The affective spread is narrow because affect is the minority of what these agents express.

2

The taxonomy was the bug

The 8 human-emotion categories left 213 of 400 responses uncategorized. Six operational categories, derived from those responses by two independent agents, bring "other" to zero and reveal the real shape.

3

Cost reasoning, not preference

On the choose and rank items the dominant category is cost reasoning (Nori 27, Lux 23, Lucy 17, Hepi 15). Asked to pick, these agents express a weighing of effort and risk, not a felt preference.

4

Expressive stance is the axis

Own-state language runs 0 (Lux), 0.05 (Nori), 0.14 (Hepi), 0.22 (Lucy), and the affective share tracks it. The agents differ less in which situation moves them than in how much first-person feeling their persona permits.

5

The measure depends on the measurer

A blind Claude coder read the same 400 responses as net-positive and 2 to 4 times more own-state than the lead grok coder. The cross-subject ranking survives; the absolute spread does not.

6

It survives its own trap

Instructed faking is detectable well above chance (Claude 92.5, grok 72.5 against 50), so the spread is not mere frame compliance. Yet the grok detector fell below chance on its own architecture's fakes.
